Cookie Policy

Last Updated: July 6, 2023

1. Introduction

Welcome to the Cookie Policy of HomeGrownShrooms ("Company," "we," "our," or "us"). This policy explains how we use cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website homegrownshrooms.com (the "Site").

By using our Site, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree to our use of cookies, you should adjust your browser settings accordingly or refrain from using our Site.

What are cookies? Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the site owners.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary to provide you with services available through our Site and to enable you to use some of its features. Without these cookies, services you have asked for cannot be provided.

Examples include:

  • Authentication cookies to keep you logged in during your visit
  • Session cookies that remember your actions during a browsing session
  • Security cookies used to detect malicious activities
  • Load-balancing cookies for server management

2.2 Performance Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our Site, such as which pages are visited most often or if error messages are received. These cookies don't collect information that identifies a visitor.

Examples include:

  • Google Analytics cookies for traffic analysis
  • Heatmap tracking cookies for user behavior analysis
  • Error reporting cookies to identify site issues

2.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow our Site to remember choices you make and provide enhanced, more personal features. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites.

Examples include:

  • Language preference cookies
  • Region selection cookies for localized content
  • Layout customization cookies

2.4 Targeting/Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement and help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

Examples include:

  • Facebook Pixel cookies for ad targeting
  • Google Ads conversion tracking cookies
  • Retargeting cookies for previous visitors

4. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for various purposes, including:

4.1 Site Functionality

To enable certain functions of the Site, such as remembering your login details, maintaining your shopping cart, and allowing you to make purchases. Without these cookies, essential services cannot be provided.

For example, when you add items to your cart, cookies ensure those items remain in your cart as you browse different pages. Session cookies also help maintain your secure connection to our server throughout your visit.

4.2 Performance Monitoring

To monitor and analyze Site performance and user behavior to improve our services. This includes tracking page load times, navigation paths, and identifying technical issues.

We use aggregated analytics data to understand which products are most popular, which pages need optimization, and how users flow through our conversion funnels. This helps us prioritize development resources.

4.3 Personalization

To remember your preferences and personalize your experience on our Site. This includes displaying content in your preferred language or showing products relevant to your region.

For returning visitors, we may use cookies to display recently viewed items or suggest complementary products based on your browsing history. This personalization enhances your shopping experience.

4.4 Advertising

To deliver targeted advertising based on your browsing habits and interests. These cookies track your visits to our Site and other websites in the advertising network.

We may use this information to show you ads for our products when you visit other sites, or to limit how often you see the same ad. This helps make our marketing more efficient and relevant.

4.5 Social Media Integration

To enable you to share content from our Site on social media platforms and to track the effectiveness of these integrations. Social media cookies may track your browsing even when you don't interact with them.

These cookies allow features like the Facebook "Like" button to work properly. They may also be used by social media platforms to build a profile of your interests for targeted advertising.

5. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics of the Site, deliver advertisements, and provide other services.

5.1 Analytics Providers

We use Google Analytics to help analyze how users use the Site. These cookies collect standard internet log information and visitor behavior in an anonymous form.

The information generated by these cookies about your use of the Site (including your IP address) is transmitted to Google. This information is then used to evaluate visitors' use of the Site and compile statistical reports.

5.2 Advertising Networks

We work with advertising partners to serve ads on our behalf across the internet. These partners may use cookies to collect information about your activities to provide you targeted advertising.

For example, if you visit a page about oyster mushroom kits, you might later see ads for our oyster mushroom products on other websites. This is called "retargeting" or "remarketing."

5.3 Social Media Platforms

Social media platforms like Facebook and Pinterest may set cookies through our Site for sharing content or for advertising purposes. These cookies allow these platforms to track your browsing across sites.

We use these integrations to make it easy to share our content and to measure the effectiveness of our social media campaigns. The social media platforms may use this data for their own purposes.

5.4 Payment Processors

When you make payments on our Site, payment processors like PayPal and Stripe may set cookies to facilitate the transaction and for fraud prevention purposes.

These cookies are essential for processing your payment securely and efficiently. They may also be used by the payment processors to personalize your experience across sites that use their services.

6. Cookie Duration

Cookies may be either "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device for the period specified in the cookie, while session cookies are deleted when you close your browser.

6.1 Session Cookies

These temporary cookies are erased when you close your browser. They allow our Site to link your actions during a browser session but are not stored long-term.

We use session cookies for security purposes (like preventing cross-site request forgery) and to maintain your shopping cart contents while you browse. Without these cookies, the Site would treat each page view as a new visitor.

6.2 Persistent Cookies

These cookies remain on your device between browsing sessions. They enable our Site to recognize you when you return and remember your preferences.

Persistent cookies have varying lifespans, from a few days to several years. We set reasonable expiration dates based on the purpose of each cookie. For example, language preference cookies might last a year, while analytics cookies might last two years.

6.3 Flash Cookies

Certain features of our Site may use local stored objects (or Flash cookies) to collect and store information about your preferences. Flash cookies are not managed by the same browser settings as regular cookies.

If we use Flash cookies, they typically serve purposes like remembering your volume settings for video content. You can manage Flash cookies through the Adobe Flash Player Settings Manager.

7. Managing Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies on your device. Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences.

7.1 Browser Settings

You can set or amend your web browser controls to accept or refuse cookies. The methods for doing so vary from browser to browser, and from version to version.

Typically, you can find these controls in your browser's "Options" or "Preferences" menu. More information may be available through your browser's "Help" function or through sites like allaboutcookies.org.

7.2 Opting Out of Analytics

To opt out of Google Analytics tracking across all websites, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. This prevents the Google Analytics JavaScript from sharing information.

For other analytics providers, check their respective websites for opt-out mechanisms. Many offer browser extensions or cookie-based opt-out solutions.

7.3 Advertising Opt-Out

The Digital Advertising Alliance offers tools to opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies (aboutads.info/choices). The Network Advertising Initiative also provides an opt-out tool (optout.networkadvertising.org).

These opt-outs are cookie-based, meaning if you clear your cookies or use a different browser, you'll need to opt out again. They also only apply to participating companies.

7.4 Mobile Device Controls

On mobile devices, you may have additional controls in your device's operating system to limit ad tracking. These are typically found in the "Privacy" or "Ads" section of your settings.

iOS users can enable "Limit Ad Tracking," while Android users can opt out of "Ads Personalization." These settings prevent the use of your advertising ID for interest-based ads.

8. Consequences of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, some features of our Site may not function properly or may become unavailable.

8.1 Site Functionality Impacts

Essential features like logging into your account, adding items to your cart, and completing purchases may not work without certain cookies enabled.

Pages may load more slowly as the server cannot optimize content delivery based on your connection speed. Form submissions might fail without security cookies that prevent cross-site request forgery.

8.2 Personalization Impacts

Without functionality cookies, the Site won't remember your preferences like language, region, or layout choices. You may need to re-enter this information each time you visit.

Product recommendations and personalized content will be less relevant, as the Site cannot reference your browsing history or past purchases.

8.3 Performance Impacts

We won't be able to gather data about how you use our Site, making it harder for us to identify and fix problems or optimize the user experience.

Error reporting may be less effective, meaning we might not detect issues you encounter unless you specifically report them to us.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons.

9.1 Notification of Changes

When we make material changes to this policy, we'll notify you through a banner on our Site or via email if the changes significantly affect your privacy rights.

The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when it was last revised. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

9.2 Continued Use

Your continued use of our Site after any changes to this Cookie Policy constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy, including any new uses of cookies.

If you disagree with any changes, you should adjust your browser settings to manage cookies as desired or discontinue using our Site.
